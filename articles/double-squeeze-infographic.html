What to Do Before July 24
01 Remodel Total Landed Cost
FOB price is now the least important number. Run total landed cost with current freight rates, current tariff schedules, current insurance premiums, and current lead times. The answer is different than it was 30 days ago.
02 Audit Every IEEPA-Era Contract
Contracts signed under the old tariff regime carry assumptions that no longer hold. Review price adjustment clauses, duty pass-through terms, and force majeure language. If your contract doesn't address tariff changes, you're absorbing the cost.
03 Accelerate Dual-Sourcing
The combined impact of tariffs and rerouting costs has changed the nearshoring math overnight. Mexico, Central America, Eastern Europe — run the comparison again with today's numbers. What didn't pencil out in January may pencil out now.
04 Use the 150-Day Window
Section 122 tariffs expire July 24 unless renewed. That's your planning horizon. Build two scenarios: one where they expire, one where they don't. Make sourcing decisions that work under both.
